  • Why this matters

It's always better to inquire than to blindly believe.

And inquiring isn't as hard as you might think.

Sometimes, it's as easy as 30 seconds on Google. Often, fact-checking websites like Snopes and Politifact have already checked to see if a story is true or not. Other times, however, you may need to do a little bit of work for yourself.

  1. If a website or social media post makes a claim, check the source. Legitimate-sounding websites are often cited as sources of information, but sounding legitimate is not enough. Often, the website is strongly biased, such as Super-PACs. Other times, it is simply not known to tell facts, such as satire news sites or a websites with a reputation for telling lies.
  2. If a photo seems fishy, you can search it on Google. Google allows users to search for other times an image appears by right-clicking it and selecting "search Google for image." Often, images and videos are taken out of context and put in unrelated stories, such as a photograph of a non-politically motivated assault attached to a fabricated story about victimization of a group for political reasons.
  3. Look for inconsistencies in a story. Fabricated news stories often have conflicting ideas or claims in them. For example, one rumor claims both that Facebook hired Snopes to fact-check the news on its site and that George Soros and the DNC are its only clients. Inconsistencies can also be found in images, such as lighting or background differences, which can indicate a photoshop job rather than a genuine image.

  5. Beyond these, there are certain red flags often found in viral claims that can be spotted right away.
    1. The author is anonymous, or claims to be a famous person.
    2. The text contains spelling and/or grammar errors, or contains an excess of capital letters and exclamation points.
    3. The post insists that it is not a hoax.
    4. If a post tells its readers "You do the math!" or something similar, it is not uncommon for the math to be wrong.
    5. The post blames mainstream news outlets for not reporting "the REAL STORY!"
